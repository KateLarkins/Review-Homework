I became a singer because of... musicals!

Throughout my elementary life, I was put in musicals, but I wouldn't act! I would only sing through solos because I was nervous, but I loved to sing!

Singing starts with practice.

When starting singing, I practiced 4 certain things. Naturally, I was never the best singer, so I had to work for it. With practicing these things, I learned how to be a better singer.

  • Breath Support
  • Music Theory
  • Posture
  • Projecting

Choir

I've done choir every year that it was offered to me in school. A choral piece is very beautiful to me and I get chills when singing one. Choir has always been a safe spot for me in school. It's a place where I can use my voice for something greater than myself.

Musicals

Musicals weren't really my passion until age 13. I still wouldn't say I am completely passionate about theater, but I do love to sing. Singing is always something I would do to express myself, especially when I am bored.

Solos

I've only ever sang on stage as a solo in musicals, and I prefer not to sing on my own when I don't have to. I believe my singing voice is either theatrical or choral, instead of as a solo. The only time I came close to singing a solo was in a duet.

A Future For Singing?

I currently do not wish to pursue a career in singing. Although I get paid to sing at my job, I do not find it's financial future promising. I find singing as a hobby and not really something to pursue. I know many others at Madison who wish to pursue it, and I believe it would be fun to sing in a college choir- but not one for a job. I am truly passionate about different things.
